# Peanut-Free Nutrition Plan for Ironman 70.3

## IMPORTANT: Peanut Allergy
⚠️ **AVOID**: Peanuts, peanut butter, peanut oil, mixed nuts (cross-contamination risk)

✅ **SAFE ALTERNATIVES**:
- Almond butter
- Sunflower seed butter (SunButter)
- Cashew butter
- Tahini (sesame seed butter)
- Soy nut butter

## Pre-Workout Snacks (60-90 min before)
- Rice cakes with almond butter and banana
- Toast with sunflower seed butter and honey
- Banana with cashew butter
- Oatmeal with almond butter
- Energy bars (check labels - no peanuts!)

## Recovery Snacks (Within 30 min post-workout)
- Chocolate milk + banana
- Greek yogurt with granola and berries
- Smoothie: banana, protein powder, almond butter, oats
- Rice cakes with almond butter and sliced apple
- Turkey wrap with hummus

## High-Carb Meals (Before Long Workouts)

### Breakfast Options
- Oatmeal with banana, almond butter, honey, cinnamon
- Whole grain toast with sunflower seed butter, banana slices
- Pancakes with maple syrup and berries
- Rice bowl with eggs, avocado, salsa
- Smoothie bowl with granola, fruit, cashew butter

### Lunch/Dinner Options
- Pasta with marinara, grilled chicken, vegetables
- Rice bowl with salmon, edamame, vegetables
- Sweet potato with grilled chicken and broccoli
- Quinoa bowl with black beans, avocado, veggies
- Stir-fry with rice, tofu/chicken, mixed vegetables

## High-Protein Recovery Meals

### Post-Long Workout Meals
- Grilled chicken with quinoa and roasted vegetables
- Salmon with rice and asparagus
- Turkey chili with beans and cornbread
- Eggs with whole grain toast, avocado, fruit
- Protein smoothie with banana, almond butter, oats, spinach

### Lean Protein Sources
- Chicken breast
- Turkey
- Fish (salmon, tuna, cod)
- Eggs
- Greek yogurt
- Cottage cheese
- Tofu/tempeh
- Beans and lentils

## Training Fuel (During Workouts >90 min)

### Commercial Options (Always Check Labels!)
- **Gels**: GU, Maurten, Huma (most are peanut-free but verify)
- **Chews**: Clif Bloks, Honey Stinger (verify no peanuts)
- **Bars**: Avoid most bars due to cross-contamination risk
  - SAFE: Rice-based bars (check labels)
  - RISKY: Most energy bars contain nuts or made in facilities with peanuts

### Whole Food Options (Safest)
- Bananas
- Dates
- Rice cakes with honey
- Homemade rice balls (onigiri)
- Fig bars (check labels)
- Applesauce pouches
- Sports drinks (all safe)

## Race Day Nutrition Plan

### 3-4 Hours Before Race
**Breakfast** (familiar, tested, 200-300g carbs):
- Large bowl of oatmeal with banana, almond butter, honey
- OR: Bagels with sunflower seed butter and jam
- OR: Rice with scrambled eggs and toast
- **Hydration**: 16-20 oz water with electrolytes

### During Race
**SWIM**: Nothing

**BIKE** (60-90g carbs/hour):
- Sports drink (20-24 oz/hour)
- 2-3 gels per hour OR
- Banana + dates + sports drink
- ⚠️ **Avoid aid station bars** - peanut contamination risk

**RUN** (30-60g carbs/hour):
- Aid station sports drink at every stop
- 1-2 gels per hour
- ⚠️ **Skip aid station bars/trail mix** - likely contain peanuts

### Post-Race Recovery
- Chocolate milk or recovery drink
- Banana
- Within 1 hour: Full meal with carbs + protein

## Weekly Meal Prep Ideas

### Batch Cook Sunday
1. **Grains**: Cook large batch of rice, quinoa, oatmeal
2. **Proteins**: Grill chicken breasts, bake salmon
3. **Vegetables**: Roast sweet potatoes, broccoli, peppers
4. **Snacks**: Prep rice cakes with almond butter, cut fruit
5. **Recovery smoothie packs**: Freeze banana, berries, spinach in bags

### Grab-and-Go Options
- Hard boiled eggs
- Greek yogurt cups
- String cheese
- Rice cakes individually wrapped
- Apples with individual almond butter packs (single-serve)
- Homemade granola (peanut-free)
- Rice balls with tuna/salmon

## Safe Brands (Always Recheck Labels)
- **Nut Butters**: Justin's almond butter, SunButter, Barney Butter
- **Energy**: Maurten gels, Huma gels (check flavors)
- **Bars**: KIND bars (check specific varieties), Larabars (some varieties)
- **Granola**: Make your own or check labels carefully

## Emergency Snack Kit (Keep in Gym Bag)
✅ Rice cakes
✅ Individual almond butter packets
✅ Bananas
✅ Sports drink mix
✅ Gels (verified peanut-free)
✅ Dates or dried fruit
✅ Protein powder (single serve)

⚠️ **Race Day Protocol**: Bring your own fuel, don't rely on aid station food
